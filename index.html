Sweet Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

¼ cup honey
2 tbsp soy sauce
½ tsp red pepper flakes
1½ tbsp olive oil
2 boneless chicken breasts (cut into bite-sized pieces)

Step-by-Step Preparation

  1. Gather all ingredients – measure honey, soy sauce, red pepper flakes, olive oil, and prepare chicken breasts.
  2. Whisk honey, soy sauce, and red pepper flakes in a small bowl until well combined; set the sauce aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chicken pieces and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned on all sides (about 5 minutes).
  4. Pour the honey mixture into the skillet with the chicken. Continue to cook and stir until chicken is no longer pink in the center and the sauce has thickened into a glossy glaze (about 5 more minutes).
  5. Serve hot immediately over rice, quinoa, or steamed vegetables. Enjoy your homemade sweet chicken!
Chef's Tip: For extra flavor, marinate chicken in soy sauce for 15 minutes before cooking.

Noodle Recipe

Ingredients

1 lb steak (cubed into 1-inch pieces)
2 tbsp butter
1 onion (diced)
2 cups beef stock
4 cups egg noodles
½ cup frozen peas
1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (slurry)
2 bay leaves
1 tsp dried thyme
Salt and pepper to taste

Step-by-Step Preparation

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat; cook steak in batches (to avoid overcrowding) until seared and browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Transfer seared steak to a plate.
  2. Melt butter in the same skillet over medium heat and sauté onion until softened, 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Add steak back to the skillet with the onion. Pour in beef stock and season with bay leaves, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring to a boil; then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until steak is tender, about 50 minutes.
  5. Stir peas and cornstarch slurry into the steak mixture; cook uncovered until the liquid thickens, about 10 minutes. Remove bay leaves.
  6. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook egg noodles in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until al dente (about 5 minutes); drain well.
  7. Serve beef mixture over noodles in deep bowls, spooning extra gravy on top. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
Chef's Note: Low and slow simmering makes the steak incredibly tender. For deeper flavor, deglaze the skillet with a splash of red wine before adding stock.

Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

8 tbsp butter (for browning)
¾ cup brown sugar
¼ cup white sugar
1 egg (room temperature)
1 tsp vanilla extract
¼ tsp salt
½ tsp baking soda
1¾ cups all-purpose flour
1 cup chocolate chips
Sea salt for sprinkling

Step-by-Step Brown Butter Cookie Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350ºF (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a pot, melt and carefully brown the butter over medium heat. Stir continuously until it turns amber and smells nutty (about 3-5 minutes). Remove from heat.
  3. Let brown butter cool until warm to the touch (about 5-10 minutes). Then add the white and brown sugar, mixing until fully incorporated.
  4. Add 1 egg at room temperature and mix again until smooth and glossy.
  5. Add the vanilla and salt onto the mixture. Then add the baking soda into the pool of vanilla before mixing it all well.
  6. Once the cookie mixture has a ribbon-like consistency, add the flour and chocolate chips, then mix gently until just combined.
  7. Once there are no more specks of flour around the batter, scoop into round dough balls (about 1.5 tbsp each) on the parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing 2 inches apart.
  8. Sprinkle with sea salt and bake for 10-12 minutes, until edges are golden brown but centers are still soft.
  9. Let sit and cool for 8-10 minutes on the baking sheet (they will continue to set). Then transfer to a wire rack and enjoy!
Baker's Tip: Brown butter adds deep caramel notes. For extra chewy cookies, chill the dough for 30 minutes before baking.

Ultimate Meal Prep Guide

Plan, prep, and store delicious meals for the week ahead.

Weekly Strategy

  • Choose 3-4 recipes with overlapping ingredients
  • Set aside 2 hours on Sunday for batch cooking
  • Pre-chop veggies & marinate proteins in advance
  • Cook grains in large batches

Storage & Containers

  • Glass containers with airtight lids
  • Separate wet & dry ingredients
  • Use portion-sized containers
  • Label everything with name & date

Freezer-Friendly Tips

  • Soups, stews, and curries freeze beautifully
  • Flash-freeze meatballs, cookie dough
  • Use silicone bags for flat freezing
  • Always cool food completely before freezing
